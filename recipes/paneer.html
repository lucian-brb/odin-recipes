Paneer butter masala

Description:

Paneer Butter Masala is a creamy, flavorful North Indian curry made with tender paneer cubes cooked in a spiced tomato and cashew gravy. The dish is enriched with butter, cream, and aromatic spices, offering a mildly sweet and savory taste. Perfect with naan or rice, this dish is a favorite for its rich texture and vibrant flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 10-12 cashews, soaked and ground to a paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Heat oil and 1 tablespoon of butter in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
  2. Add the finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  5. Mix in the cashew paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add the paneer cubes and gently mix them into the gravy.
  7. Pour in the fresh cream and add sugar (if using). Stir well to combine.
  8. Simmer the curry for 5-7 minutes on low heat, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors.
  9. Turn off the heat and stir in the remaining tablespoon of butter for a rich finish.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
